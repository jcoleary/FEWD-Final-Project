Africa

What You Need to Know

Important as the continent continues to develop. Rich in natural resources, espescially precious metals. Major investment in continent from China. Fast growing population. Strong Islamic State prescense in Egypt and Libya.

Fast Facts :

  • Area: 30,300,000 km2 (11,700,000 mi2)
  • Total Population: 1,100,000,000
  • Climate: Arid at nothern and southern subtropical highs, tropical in central Africa, subarctic at high peaks.
  • Number of countries: 54
  • Largest Countries (by population): Nigeria, Ethiopia, Democratic Republic of the Congo, South Africa, Tanzania

Discrepancies:

Western Sahara is not considered an independent entity of Morocco on this website. The Islamic State(IS)/ISIS/ISIL is not considered a legitimate nation state

Asia

What You Need to Know

Contains largest segment of human population. Rich in natural resources, especially rare earth metals and hydrocarbons (coal, oil, natural gas). China is the world's second largest economy and has largest standing army. Investment from foreign nations expected to increase. Japan's population is rapidly shrinking. Strong Islamic State prsecense in Iraq, Syria, Bangladesh, and Pakistan.

Fast Facts:

  • Area: 44,579,000 km2 (17,212,000 mi2)
  • Total Population: 4,164,252,000
  • Climate: Arid in Near East and North of China (Gobi Desert), tropical and wet on Indian subcontient and southeast, dry throughout central Asian plateau, temperate throughout China proper, subartic and arctic boreal forest through Siberia, arctic and dry over Hindu Kush and Himalayas.
  • Number of countries: 49
  • Largest Countries (by population): China, India, Indonesia, Pakistan, Bangladesh

Discrepancies:

Taiwan is considered an independent entity and not a province of China on this website. Turkey and Russia are counted twice as they exist in both Europe and Asia. The Islamic State(IS)/ISIS/ISIL is not considered a legitimate nation state.

Australia-Pacific

What You Need to Know

Australia and New Guinea are rich in resources such as iron and rubber. Australia and New Zealand are considered both promoters of peace and buffers in the region against China and other continental Asian nations.

Fast Facts:

  • Area:8,525,989 km2 (3,291,903 mi2)
  • Total Population: 36,659,000
  • Climate: Mainly tropical throughout Pacific islands and Australia's eastern coast. Arid throughout Western and central Australia. Mainly temperate throughout New Zealand.
  • Number of countries: 14
  • Largest Countries (by population): Australia, Papua New Guinea, New Zealand, Fiji, Solomon Islands

Discrepancies:

12 Pacific island nations are included here and not in Asia.

Europe

What You Need to Know

The European Union (EU) is currently the world's largest collective economy, which is expected to change once the United Kingdom officially leaves. There is a large demographic shift taking place in Europe, particlarly with an influx of migrants from Africa and the Middle East. Native population is shrinking throughout Europe. Tension continues to grow between US-backed NATO member states and Russia.

Fast Facts:

  • Area: 10,180,000 km2 (3,930,000 mi2)
  • Total Population: 742,452,000
  • Climate: Mostly temperate, some artic climate in northern reaches of Scandaniva, warm along the Mediterranean coast.
  • Number of countries: 47
  • Largest Countries (by population): Russia, Germany, Turkey, France, United Kingdom

    • Discrepancies:

    Cyprus is considered part of Europe on this website. Turkey and Russia are counted twice as they exist in both Europe and Asia. Turkey and Russia rank as some of the largest nations in Europe due to the fact that their major population centers are in what is considered "Europe." Kosovo is recognized as an indepndent entity on this website.

North America

What You Need to Know

North America is very rich in natural resources. The United States is the world's largest single nation economy and considered the world's most powerful country. The US hold's the world's largest reserves of coal. Canadian-American trade is the largest flow of capital in the world.

Fast Facts:

  • Area: 24,709,000 km2 (9,540,000 mi2)
  • Total Population: 565,265,000
  • Climate: Mostly temperate, arctic in Northern Canada and Alaska, warm to subtropical in Central America, Hawaii, and the Caribbean.
  • Number of countries: 23
  • Largest Countries (by population): United States of America, Mexico, Canada, Guatemala, Cuba

    • Discrepancies:

    Central America and Caribbean are considered part of North America and included in country count.

South America

What You Need to Know

South America is rich in gold, silver, tin, copper, and iron ore. Politically, most South American countries have elected leftist leaders in recent decades. The continent is rapidly developing and has employed protectionist policies to encourage local development. Brazil is by far the region's largest country and is hosting the Rio 2016 Summer Olympics. Political corruption, delays, and the threat of Zika virus has seriously hindered construction.

Fast Facts:

  • Area: 17,840,000 km2 (6,890,000 mi2)
  • Total Population: 385,742,554
  • Climate: Extremley diverse. Rainforest throughout Brazil, Ecuador, Colombia, Venezuela, Guyana, Suriname, Peru, Bolivia, and Paraguay. North and central South America is genrally humid and wet. Mountainous on Western Coast through Chile, Peru, and Argentina. Temperate through Uruguay and nothern Argentina, while arid and subpolar in the South.
  • Number of countries: 12
  • Largest Countries (by population):Brazil, Colombia, Argentina, Venzuela, Peru.

Discrepancies:

French Guiana and the Falkland Islands are considered parts of France and the UK, respectively, and are therefore not inculded in the country count.
